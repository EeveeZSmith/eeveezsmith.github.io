Absolute Length Units

Absolute length units are those that exist as a definite, independent unit of measurement. Regardless of how or where they are used, they appear as a standard, constant size.

Example: 1 pixel (px) is always 1/96th of 1 inch.

Relative Length Units

Relative length units are those whose measurement is determined in relation to something else, such as another object on the page or a percentrage of the screen on which the page is viewed.

Example: Viewport width (vw) tells an object to take up a percentage of the device's screen.

A Sampling of Length Units

  • Pixel (px) = 1/96th of 1 inch. A commonly used absolute measurement for images and other objects.
  • Point (pt) = 1/72nd of 1 inch. Absolute measurement typically used for font sizes.
  • Viewport width (vw) = Width relative to the viewscreen. 10vw would be 10% of the width of the screen.
  • Viewport height (vh) = Height relative to the viewscreen. 10vh would be 10% of the height of the screen.
  • Inch (in) = 96 pixels. Common, everyday absolute measurement of length, height, width, or depth.
  • Pica (pc) = 1/6th of 1 inch. Less common absolute measurement.
