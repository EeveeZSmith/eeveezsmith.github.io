General Sherman

General Sherman, located within California’s Sequoia National Park, is the world’s largest tree!

It measures 275 feet in height, and its trunk has a circumference of 103 feet at the base.

It is not the tallest tree in the world, but it is the largest by volume, taking up more than 52 thousand cubic feet of space and weighing approximately 4.2 million pounds.

It is estimated to have been seeded between 700 and 300 BCE, which makes it at least a few hundred years older than Julius Caesar.
